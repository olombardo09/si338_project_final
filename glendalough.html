A Traveler's Guide to Glendalough

What I Did

  • Travel: 1.5-hour bus ride
  • Activities: Hiking, Historical Sites, Swimming
  • Time: ~4 hours

For 23 euros round-trip, we took the St. Kevin's bus to Wicklow Mountains National Park, just a bit over an hour south of Dublin. It is famous for its gorgeous views, ancient monastic sites, and abundant wildlife. We arrived shortly after noon and decided to hike the shorter Spinc route, which is 6km and takes approximately 2 hours. The terrain for this specific hike consists of rough mountain tracks and forest roads, but it offers a great view of the upper lake.

We took our time on this hike, taking lots of pictures and eating our packed lunches. After we finished the loop, we still had about 1.5 hours remaining, so we explored the Poulanass route, which is almost 2km long. This loop is not as strenuous and goes past the Poulanass Waterfall.

With less than an hour until our bus ride back, we wandered around the lower lake as well as Glendalough's Monastic Site, founded by St. Kevin in the latter part of the 6th century. This has become one of the most important monastic sites in Ireland, withstanding many targeted attacks from local tribes and Norse Invaders. We joined in on a tour around the site, learning about the Round Tower. Our day trip ended by walking back to the Visitor's Center and taking the bus back to Dublin!

Recommendations

  • Hiking: Wicklow Mountains National Park is a wonderful place for hiking. There are 9 different color-marked trails of varying terrain, difficult, and length. To see the most amount of wildlife and view the lakes from the highest point, take the red Spinc and Wicklow Way route. It is 11.5km and will take approximately 4.5 hours so plan accordingly.
  • Cycling: Cyclists are welcome on the park's hard, surfaced roads and selected walking trails only. The trails that are suitable for cyclists include the Green Route (around the lower lake), the Purple Route (down the Miners' Road, as far as the Miners' Village, and back the same way), and the Orange Route (around the Derrybawn Woodland Trail).
  • Picnics & Barbeques: Picnic areas are located at the Upper Lake in Glendalough. For safety & environmental reasons, only gas barbeques are allowed. Fires and solid fuel barbeques are not to be used.
