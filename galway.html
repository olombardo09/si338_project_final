A Traveler's Guide to Galway

What I Did

  • Travel: 3-hour bus ride from Dublin
  • Activities: Market, Live Music, Sightseeing
  • Time: 3 days

This city is full of things to do. After briefly checking into our hostel, we set out into town to explore. We first stopped at the Galway Market, which is perfect for supporting local vendors and a great place to purchase souvenirs like thrifted clothing, handcrafted Celtic jewelry, and prints. We headed into the Latin Quarter to do some shopping, grab food, and buy our Claddagh rings! We got ours from Thomas Dillon's, who claim to be the original makers of the gold Claddagh rings.

After a quick food stop at HighCafé, we chilled at the hostel before exploring Galway's Cathedral and walking along the river. The following morning, I stopped by the infamous Long Walk with its colorful houses and found my way out to Mutton Island along the pier. At night, we checked out The Quays Bar & Restaurant, which had a great atmosphere with live music.

For our class site visit, we went to the Computer and Communications Museum of Ireland. Our speaker, Brendan, gave us a wonderful overview of the evolution of technology that has shaped Ireland's technological landscape, celebrating the contributions of Irish inventors and innovators. We got to explore some of the tech in the museum and even played a video game!

Where to Eat

  • Coffeewerk and Press: coffee, pasteries
  • The Dough Bros: pizza
  • McDonagh's: fish & chips
  • The King's Head: Irish, Bar; locally sourced

Recommendations

  • Outdoor Activities: Rent a bike and cycle along the River Corrib path, which takes you from the heart of the city past the Galway Cathedral and up toward NUI Galway and Menlo Castle. For a longer coastal ride, you can cycle all the way out to Mutton Island or along the Salthill Promenade.
  • Shopping & Culture: Explore The Latin Quarter, famous for its cobblestone streets, street performers, and vibrant pubs. You'll find Charlie Byrne's Bookshop, a combination of new and secondhand books. On weekends, don't miss the historic Galway Market right in Eyre Square to browse handcrafted goods and grab local street food.
  • Museum: Visit the Computer and Communications Museum of Ireland located at the Insight Centre for Data Analytics. It features a collection of vintage tech, from early radio and telecom gear to classic computers and video games. It offers an intriguing look at the Irish inventors and innovators who helped shape the global technological landscape.
