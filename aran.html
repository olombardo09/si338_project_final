A Traveler's Guide to Inis Mór

What I Did

  • Travel: 45-min bus from Galway & 40-min ferry from Rossaveel
  • Activities: 10-mile bike ride (Out-and-back)
  • Time: ~6 hours total

Through Aran Island Ferries, a group of us booked a day trip to Inishmore, the largest of the Aran Islands. For €38, we caught a 45-minute bus ride from Galway to Rossaveel, where we hopped on a ferry over to the island. Upon arrival, we rented bikes to explore. While there are two main bike paths of varying difficulty, we opted for the easier route. The ride offers amazing ocean views alongside stone walls, ancient forts, grazing livestock, and even a seal colony if you're lucky.

The path ends at Dún Aonghasa, the oldest prehistoric stone fort perched on the cliff's edge. Admission is just €3 for students. After an uphill 20-minute walk to the fort, we took photos and enjoyed a quick lunch on the grass. Back at the base, we browsed the Aran Sweater Market before cycling back to the main town. We ended our afternoon returning the bikes, grabbing a chai latte, and strolling along the beach, where we watched local teens playing hurling and camogie, and a few brave souls even swimming in the bay, before catching our 4:00 PM ferry back to the mainland.

Where to Eat

  • Joe Watty's Pub & Seafood Bar: traditional Irish
  • Man of Aran Fudge: Fudge desserts
  • Aran Islands Cafe: drinks & baked goods

Recommendations

  • Cycling: To cover the most ground during your stay, at Aran Bike Hire you're able to rent a bike (manual, electric, tandem) for 20 euros/day. Starting at Kilmurvey Beach, you can ride along the north side road with stunning ocean and animal views.
  • Swimming: At Kilmurvey Beach, you'll find clear waters with soft, sandy beaches. Many vistors will pack lunch and head down 7km to the beach from the main village. It's perfect for both new and experienced swimmers as there is no strong current.
  • See the Seals: About a mile past Kilmurvey Beach, you'll be able to find a grey seal colony. It has been noted to see up to 25 seals at a time here sunbathing on the rocks. The viewpoint has pinic benches which are perfect for relaxing with a great view.
