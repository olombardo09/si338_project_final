A Traveler's Guide to Howth

What I Did

  • Travel: 35m DART from Dublin
  • Hike: 6.2 miles (Out-and-back)
  • Time: ~4 hours total

Just 35 minutes from Dublin, I arrived in Howth with a friend for the afternoon. We opted to do the Howth Cliff Loop, bypassing the full loop for an out-and-back route along the seaside to keep the water in view the entire time. To get there we walked through town, past the market, and up to the trailhead. Our modified route took approximately 6.2 miles and took 2.5 hours. The path is well-maintained and accessible for most fitness levels. There are plenty of stone ledges and benches for a quick break! It did get super windy at times so even if it's sunny, bring layers. The highlight of the hike was defintely near the lighthouse- the views are incredible. After finishing the hike, we headed back to the docks to eat our packed lunches on a bench by the boats. To warm up, we went to the Howth Market and grabbed coffees from Bodega while shopping around for handmade gifts, knick-knacks, and baked goods. After about 4 hours of exploring, we hopped back on the train to Dublin. It's a short trip, but 100% worth it!

Where to Eat

  • The Pier House: Irish, Seafood
  • Beshoff Bros: takeway fish & chips
  • O'Connell's Howth: Irish, Bar

Recommendations

  • Hiking: Howth is a part of the Dublin Coastal Trail and is great for those who want to stroll along East Pier to the lighthouse or experienced hikers who would enjoy Howth Head. There are four different hiking options, all of various distances and views of the town.
  • Outdoor Activities: If you want to be closer to the water, you may enjoy kayaking with Shearwater Sea to explore caves and cliffs. For those who want to sit back and relax rather than paddling, tours are available to book with Ireland's Eye Ferries or Howth Cliff Cruises. Additionally, Howth Yacht Club offers non-members interested in sailing the chance to learn to sail and drive a powerboat. For golfers, Howth Golf Club is available for play.
  • Museums: Ye Olde Hurdy-Gurdy Museum of Vintage Radio is located in Martello Tower and only a short walk off Main Street. There are collections of old radios, music boxes, and gramophones. You can also visit the National Transport Museum which displays rare and old vehicles.
