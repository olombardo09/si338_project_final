A Traveler's Guide to Dublin

What I Did

  • Travel: Flight from Detroit to Dublin
  • Activities: Sightseeing, Guided Tours, Museums, Food
  • Time: ~3 weeks

Staying right in Dublin's City Centre, there are loads of things to do and see. Over the course of the trip, I explored many parts of the city. Some of my favorite activities included running through Phoenix Park, visiting EPIC - The Irish Emigration Museum, touring the Docklands, book shopping, and getting coffee and pastries at local cafes. We also visited the Guinness Storehouse, which was a fantastic, interactive museum experience to learn more about the history of the company.

Dublin has an incredible food scene, and a few of my favorite spots for a morning brew were Beanhive, Gerard's, and Rumi. For dinner on our first night, we ate at The Brazen Head, one of the oldest pubs in Dublin, which offers a great atmosphere and excellent food options. I tried their Bangers & Mash, and it was absolutely delicious. If you have a sweet tooth, there are plenty of spots for a treat, including Gino's Gelato, The Rolling Donut, and various local bakeries.

To squeeze in as much sightseeing as possible, we took a Dublin Bus tour with stops at St. Stephen's Green, Collins Barracks, and Phoenix Park. We also did a tour of the Docklands, visited the National History Museum, stopped by the Irish Wheelchair Association (IWA), walked the historic grounds of Trinity College, and even checked out the Google Headquarters.

Where to Eat & Drink in Dublin

Dublin's food scene is a great mix of historic pubs, cozy coffee spots, and incredible bakeries. Based on my three weeks exploring the city centre, here are my favorite places to grab a bite:

Best Cafes for Coffee & Pastries:

  • Beanhive: My absolute favorite spot for a latte or london fog. The coffee is 10/10, and their pastries are the perfect way to start a day of sightseeing
  • Gerard's: This is a good spot for a takeaway coffee and treats on your way to class
  • Rumi: Another great cafe if you are looking for a quality coffee and a relaxing space to work

Pubs & Hearty Dinners:

  • The Brazen Head: One of the oldest pubs in Dublin, and a must-visit on your trip
  • Doom Slice: Pizza & Drinks
  • Darkey Kelly's: Irish Pub

Sweet Treats:

  • Gino's Gelato: Crepes & Gelato
  • The Rolling Donut: Gourmet Donuts

Recommendations

  • Book of Kells: The Long Room Library at Trinity college is great for literary fans or someone who enjoys a good library. It is home to the Book of Kells which is a complete gospels manuscript. You can also check out the Book of Kells Experience, a digital exhibit where you can immerse yourself and learn even more.
  • The Guinness Storehouse Experience: For 22 euros per person, you can explore the home of Ireland's most iconic beer. The tour is very interactive and immersive with lots of moving digital displays. It concludes at the Gravity Bar where you get a 360 view of Dublin and a free pint.
  • Walk around The Liberties: This is one of the most historic neighborhoods in Dublin surrounding the Guinness Storehouse. You'll find Christ Church Cathedral, the Dublinia Viking & Medieval Museum, and lots of great pubs!
  • Phoenix Park: This is a large public park in Dublin where you'll find lots of wild deer, the Irish President's residency, and the Dublin Zoo. It is a great place to run, cycle, or chill on a bench and read.
